Frequently Asked Questions

IS IT POSSIBLE TO APPLY AS A PROGRAM OR INITIATIVE WITHIN AN ORGANIZATION?

Yes. Any program or initiative with an operating budget between $100,000 and $2 million may apply. In these cases, the program director as well as a representative from the parent organization must be committed to participating in Social Innovation Forum meetings and events. In addition, the entire application must refer to the program, not the parent organization (budget numbers, performance metrics, etc.). Please note that there are different requirements for the “Anything Goes” track. Please see “Anything Goes” track descriptions for details.

IF APPLYING TO MORE THAN ONE SOCIAL ISSUE TRACK, MUST AN ORGANIZATION SUBMIT MORE THAN ONE APPLICATION?

In general, yes. Each track has its own set of evaluators who are experts on that particular social issue. While a fair amount of information may be copied and pasted from one application to the next, we ask that multiple-track applicants tailor their answers to some of the more thoughtful, paragraph-response questions to demonstrate their “fit” with each of those tracks. Our evaluators like to see that applicants have thought about how their organizations address specific social issues and what makes them innovators in those spaces. With two sustainability tracks this year, we expect that some groups will apply to both, although we encourage you to read the track paragraphs carefully and to apply for the track where you feel your organization is the strongest fit. For the “Anything Goes” track, no. Organizations that apply for other social issue tracks will not be considered for the “Anything Goes” track.

WHAT IS THE APPLICATION PROCESS FOR THOSE WHO APPLIED DURING A PREVIOUS CYCLE OF THE SOCIAL INNOVATION FORUM AND WERE NOT SELECTED?

The application itself changes from year to year. So that we have consistent information across applicants, we insist that all applicants for this year’s cycle complete a 2014–2015 application. However, it is sometimes possible to copy and paste responses from a previous application into corresponding sections of the current year’s application. To assist you, we are happy to provide copies of your past application(s) upon request.

WHAT ARE OUTPUTS AND OUTCOMES?

Outputs are the direct, short-term results of an organization’s activities. Outputs are often helpful indicators of the size and/or scope of an organization’s work.
For example:

  • Number of participants or beneficiaries
  • Number of volunteers
  • Number of programs offered
  • Percentage of participants completing the program
  • Average number of program hours per participant

Outcomes are units of measurement that assess an organization’s long-term results, or progress in addressing its target social problem.
For example:

  • Percentage of participants demonstrating improved conflict resolution skills
  • Percentage of participants who graduate from high school
  • Percentage of participants employed upon program completion

We recognize that measuring outcomes can be challenging and that many organizations are still in the early stages of developing tools and processes to gather information. For this section of the application, you may list measurements that the leadership team would like to use to measure outcomes in the future.

I AM HAVING TROUBLE WITH MY APPLICATION ONLINE. WHAT AM I DOING WRONG?

Before filling out the online application, we recommend writing the longer responses in Microsoft Word or a similar program and using the “Word Count” feature to check that your responses do not exceed our character limit suggestions (which do include spaces). Before submitting your application, also check that email addresses, phone numbers, and other important fields have been input accurately. The Social Innovation Forum staff is available to help you through all sorts of technological challenges, so please do not hesitate to call or email us as you run into issues! You can reach us at socialinnovationforum@rootcause.org or 617.649.1518.
